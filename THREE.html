GREENLAND SHARKS

Greenland sharks may live 250–400 years — and it changes how their mind works. Greenland sharks grow and age so slowly that their brains develop over centuries. Psychology implication: their perception of time, learning, and stress is radically different from fast-lived animals.

  • Very slow emotional reactivity
  • Minimal stress response
  • Long-term memory without urgency

AXOLOTLS

Axolotls don’t just regenerate limbs — they may not experience trauma the same way. Axolotls can regenerate brain tissue, spinal cord, and even parts of the heart without forming scar tissue.

  • Minimal long-term fear conditioning
  • No permanent trauma encoding
  • Nervous system biased toward reset rather than rumination

This makes them a major model organism for studying PTSD prevention, neural plasticity, and memory rewriting.
